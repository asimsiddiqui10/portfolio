2025

Triad

Founding AI Engineer — product and platform.

Triad is an early-stage platform focused on the mental and emotional development of young athletes. It helps players, parents, and coaches build healthier habits around confidence, reflection, and communication — not just performance.

Triad is designed around a privacy-first knowledge graph that models relationships between athletes, reflections, habits, and contextual signals over time, rather than relying on raw conversational history. Sensitive data is stored as structured, permissioned nodes with explicit ownership and visibility rules across athlete, parent, and coach roles. The knowledge graph enables longitudinal insight and personalization while minimizing data exposure, and AI systems operate only on scoped, derived representations rather than direct access to raw user content. This architecture emphasizes auditability, least-privilege access, and long-term trust, which are critical when building systems for youth users.

2025

Sporttriad

Co-founding team — product research, product design, and UX.

Sporttriad is the company behind Triad. As part of the founding team, I led product research to understand how young athletes, parents, and coaches actually talk about stress, confidence, and performance. This meant running interviews and lightweight discovery sessions, mapping out the emotional journey around games and training, and turning those narratives into specific opportunities for the product to help.

On the product design side, I owned the end-to-end UX for our first releases: onboarding, daily check-ins, insights, and coach dashboards. I worked from low-fidelity flows to detailed UI, creating a design system that balanced a calm, trustworthy aesthetic with clear affordances for logging feelings, reflecting on patterns, and taking action. Every interaction was designed to feel supportive rather than clinical, especially for younger athletes who might be opening up about difficult topics for the first time.

Beyond the UI, I helped define how Sporttriad ran experiments: what to ship first, how to measure whether a feature actually reduced friction or improved reflection habits, and how to keep the system technically simple while still supporting future AI-assisted experiences. Day to day, this looked like an ongoing loop between user research, product strategy, IA and interaction design, and hands-on implementation in collaboration with engineering.

marketise.ai

Frontend Engineer + AI

Built with scalable backend services and modular AI components, the system ingests brand profiles or URLs and produces structured insights about visual identity, messaging, and aesthetic cues that can inform design and content decisions. Soccertact

Product design | UI/UX

I designed a scouting dashboard focused on helping users quickly understand player information without cognitive overload. The work was driven by user research and regular feedback from scouts to understand how they scan data, what they trust visually, and where friction exists in existing tools. The goal was to surface the right information at the right moment, reducing unnecessary complexity while keeping depth accessible when needed.

From a design perspective, I focused on clear information hierarchy, consistent spacing, and readable typography to support fast decision-making. Layouts and components were designed and iterated in Figma, with attention to alignment, color usage, and interaction states across different screen sizes. The final UI emphasizes clarity, consistency, and usability, ensuring the dashboard feels intuitive and reliable in real scouting workflows.
